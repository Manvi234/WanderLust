London

Absolutely, here's a sample 7-day itinerary for London. Keep in mind that this is just a suggestion and you can adjust it based on your interests and preferences. London is a vibrant city with a lot to offer, so make sure to check opening hours and availability of attractions in advance.

Day 1: Arrival and Introduction

- Morning: Arrival in London, check into your accommodation.
- Afternoon: Take a leisurely stroll along the South Bank of the River Thames, visiting landmarks like the London Eye and the Shard. - Evening: Enjoy a traditional English dinner at a local pub.

Day 2: Classic London

- Morning: Visit the iconic Tower of London to explore its history and see the Crown Jewels.
- Afternoon: Cross Tower Bridge and visit Borough Market for lunch.
- Late Afternoon: Explore the historic area of Westminster, including Big Ben, Houses of Parliament, and Westminster Abbey.
- Evening: Take a scenic Thames River cruise to see the city's landmarks illuminated.

Day 3: Museums and Parks

- Morning: Visit the British Museum to see its vast collection of artifacts.
- Afternoon: Explore Covent Garden for shopping and lunch.
- Late Afternoon: Relax in Hyde Park or stroll through Kensington Gardens.
- Evening: Attend a West End theatre show.

Day 4: Royal London

- Morning: Tour Buckingham Palace (when open) or watch the Changing of the Guard ceremony.
- Afternoon: Visit the Churchill War Rooms to learn about the history of World War II.
- Late Afternoon: Explore the upscale shopping district of Knightsbridge, including Harrods.
- Evening: Enjoy a fine dining experience in one of London's renowned restaurants.

Day 5: East London and Markets

- Morning: Explore the vibrant and artistic area of Shoreditch.
- Afternoon: Visit Columbia Road Flower Market (Sundays) or Brick Lane Market (Sundays) for shopping and street food.
- Late Afternoon: Discover the unique street art in the surrounding streets.
- Evening: Experience the diverse culinary scene in Brick Lane.

Day 6: Day Trip

- Morning: Take a day trip to Windsor Castle or Oxford (both are around an hour away by train).
- Afternoon: Explore the chosen destination, whether it's the historic town of Windsor or the renowned university city of Oxford.
- Evening: Return to London and relax or do some last-minute shopping.

Day 7: Thameside Sights

- Morning: Visit the Victoria and Albert Museum for art and design.
- Afternoon: Explore the scenic area of Greenwich, including the Royal Observatory and the Cutty Sark.
- Late Afternoon: Enjoy a walk along the Thames Path and relax by the river.
- Evening: Have a farewell dinner at a riverside restaurant.

Remember that this itinerary is just a starting point. London offers a wealth of attractions and experiences, so feel free to customize your itinerary based on your interests and the time you have available.
