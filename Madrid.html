Madrid

Here's a suggested 7-day itinerary for exploring Madrid, Spain. This itinerary provides a mix of cultural, historical, culinary, and leisure activities to give you a well-rounded experience of the city. Feel free to adjust it based on your interests and the opening hours of attractions.

Day 1: Arrival and Introduction

- Morning: Arrive in Madrid, check in to your accommodation, and get settled.
- Afternoon: Take a leisurely stroll around the historic center. Visit Puerta del Sol, Plaza Mayor, and Mercado de San Miguel.
- Evening: Enjoy your first Spanish dinner at a traditional tapas bar in La Latina neighborhood.

Day 2: Art and Museums

- Morning: Visit the world-renowned Museo del Prado. Explore its impressive collection of European art, including works by Goya, Velázquez, and El Greco.
- Afternoon: Head to the Thyssen-Bornemisza Museum, which features a diverse collection of art spanning different periods and styles.
- Evening: Relax at Retiro Park. Rent a rowboat on the lake or simply enjoy a leisurely walk through the park's beautiful pathways.

Day 3: Royal Madrid

- Morning: Visit the Royal Palace (Palacio Real) and its adjacent Almudena Cathedral. Explore the grandeur and history of these architectural wonders.
- Afternoon: Explore the Sabatini Gardens and stroll through Plaza de España.
- Evening: Indulge in a fine dining experience at a restaurant that offers Spanish cuisine with a modern twist.

Day 4: Madrid of the Arts

- Morning: Visit the Reina Sofía Museum, home to Picasso's famous "Guernica" among other contemporary art pieces.
- Afternoon: Explore the Lavapiés neighborhood, known for its vibrant street art scene and multicultural atmosphere.
- Evening: Enjoy a flamenco show at a local tablao to experience this passionate Spanish dance and music form.

Day 5: Day Trip to Toledo

- Take a day trip to the nearby city of Toledo, known for its medieval architecture and historical significance. Explore the Alcázar, Toledo Cathedral, and the charming streets of the old town.

Day 6: Food and Shopping

- Morning: Start your day at the San Miguel Market, where you can sample a variety of Spanish foods and delicacies.
- Afternoon: Explore Gran Vía, a major shopping street with a mix of boutiques, international brands, and theaters.
- Evening: Dine at a traditional mesón to savor authentic Spanish dishes like paella, jamón ibérico, and churros.

Day 7: Madrid's Modern Side

- Morning: Visit the Matadero Madrid, a former slaughterhouse turned cultural center with exhibitions, events, and contemporary art installations.
- Afternoon: Explore the Malasaña neighborhood, known for its alternative vibe, vintage shops, and trendy cafes.
- Evening: Wrap up your trip with a rooftop sunset view from places like Círculo de Bellas Artes or The Roof at Mercado de San Antón.

Remember that this itinerary is just a suggestion, and you can tailor it to your preferences and interests.
